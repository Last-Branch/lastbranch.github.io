About the Project

Scribit is a real-time communication assistance tool designed to reduce barriers between individuals with hearing and/or speaking impairments and people who do not know sign language. The system aims to generate low-latency live subtitles by combining multiple modalities: camera-based lip reading, automatic speech recognition (speech-to-text), and Turkish Sign Language (TSL) recognition.

By leveraging both visual and auditory cues, Scribit improves transcription robustness in challenging conditions such as noisy environments or situations where audio is unavailable. The planned architecture uses MediaPipe for facial/gesture tracking in both lip-reading and sign language recognition scenarios, Whisper for speech-to-text conversion, and and custom trained deep learning models for Turkish Sign Language recognition, with a modular design that can be extended to additional languages and sign languages in future iterations.
